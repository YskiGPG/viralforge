# Within-subjects user study — results (Phase 5.4)

Two creators ran the protocol in `user_study_protocol.md` (within-subjects, counterbalanced).
n = 2, within the stated n = 2–5 range. Qualitative only — we report patterns and quotes, not
statistics.

> **Scope boundary (unchanged).** This measures the *planning output*, not published videos.
> Neither participant filmed, posted, or tracked real views. Every claim below compares the two
> *plans* a person produced; none attributes real-world video performance to the system.

## Captured sessions

**Participant 1** — order: **Unaided → ViralForge**

| Condition | Idea | Time | Confidence (1–5) | Ready to film (1–5) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Unaided | Cold brew coffee tutorial | 9 min | 3 | 2 |
| ViralForge | Iced matcha tutorial | 5 min | 5 | 4 |

- *Unaided notes:* trouble deciding how to start and what shots to include; came up with a few
  title ideas but wasn't sure they were interesting enough.
- *ViralForge notes:* liked having a hook and shot sequence already suggested; easier to imagine
  filming right away; spent less time on titles.
- *Trusts more:* ViralForge — "felt more structured and gave clearer ideas for how to open the
  video and keep viewers watching." *Would use:* yes, especially when unsure how to start.
- *Quote:* "The AI basically gave me a starting point so I didn't have to stare at a blank page."

**Participant 2** — order: **ViralForge → Unaided**

| Condition | Idea | Time | Confidence (1–5) | Ready to film (1–5) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| ViralForge | Beginner rock climbing tips | 4 min | 4 | 4 |
| Unaided | Morning productivity routine | 8 min | 3 | 3 |

- *ViralForge notes:* the hook and title suggestions were helpful; clear direction and sequence
  of shots.
- *Unaided notes:* could think of scenes to record but had trouble deciding what would make the
  video stand out; needed more time to organize ideas.
- *Trusts more:* ViralForge — "gave more specific guidance and made the videos feel more
  complete." *Would use:* probably; "would still change some things, but it saved time."
- *Quote:* "I wouldn't follow everything exactly, but it got me 80% of the way there."

## Summary

| Participant | Order | Unaided time | ViralForge time | Confidence (unaided → assisted) | Ready (unaided → assisted) | Preferred |
|---|---|---:|---:|:--:|:--:|---|
| P1 | Unaided → ViralForge | 9 min | 5 min | 3 → 5 | 2 → 4 | ViralForge |
| P2 | ViralForge → Unaided | 8 min | 4 min | 3 → 4 | 3 → 4 | ViralForge |
| **Mean** | — | **8.5 min** | **4.5 min** | **3.0 → 4.5** | **2.5 → 4.0** | ViralForge (2/2) |

## Analysis

- **Planning time roughly halved** with ViralForge (8.5 → 4.5 min mean, ≈47% faster), and the
  effect held in **both counterbalance orders**. This matters: P2 used ViralForge *first* and was
  still faster on the assisted idea than on the later unaided one, so the speed-up isn't just a
  practice/order effect — if it were, the second (unaided) task would have been the fast one.
- **Confidence and readiness rose** in the assisted condition for both participants (mean
  confidence 3.0 → 4.5, readiness 2.5 → 4.0).
- **The mechanism, in their words, is structure.** Both unaided notes describe the same friction —
  not lacking *ideas* but lacking a way to *start and sequence* them ("trouble deciding how to
  start and what shots," "trouble deciding what would make the video stand out"). The assisted
  notes credit exactly what the pipeline produces: a ready hook + shot sequence + titles. The
  blank-page quote ("a starting point so I didn't have to stare at a blank page") is the clearest
  read on what the tool removes.
- **Honest dissent / ceiling.** Neither treated the plan as final — "I wouldn't follow everything
  exactly… 80% of the way there." ViralForge is read as a strong *starting point*, not a finished
  script. No case had unaided beat assisted.

## Limitations

n = 2, qualitative and self-reported, sessions run by the project team, ideas not professionally
produced. We report this as a directional pattern (faster planning, higher self-rated
confidence/readiness, unanimous preference), not a statistical result, and we keep the
plans-not-videos boundary on every claim.
